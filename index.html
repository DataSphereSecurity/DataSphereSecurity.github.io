BACKGROUND

Mitchell Warlick is a respected veteran advocate for the environment and indigenous communities worldwide, with a career spanning over 25 years. He is dedicated to promoting justice, equality, and peace and has provided valuable investigative resources to support the work of journalists.

During the late 1990s, Warlick worked closely with indigenous tribes in the Amazon Rain Forest, helping to amplify their voices and thwart efforts by corporations to exploit the area for profit. His Efforts helped prevent the destruction of old-growth forests and unethical sourcing.

In the early 2000s, Warlick uncovered fraudulent claims by the U.S. government about the presence of weapons of mass destruction in Iraq. He also documented the abuse and torture of political prisoners in the Middle East, exposing a pattern of "extraordinary rendition" that was later condemned by human rights organizations worldwide.

More recently, Warlick has become involved in efforts to end state-sponsored police brutality, organizing and participating in marches to demand change.

Warlick is currently working on a documentary project, "Jane Like Me: On the Front Lines with the Warriors for Reproductive Justice," which explores the U.S. Supreme Court's challenge to individuals' right to control their own bodies.
